# ---------------- MEMBRANE MATERIALS ----------------

Q: pvdf, hydrophilic pvdf
A: PVDF (hydrophilic) offers low protein binding and ultra-low extractables - ideal for
   LC-MS and sensitive analyses, plus broad chemical compatibility. In cartridge form,
   integrity-tested pore sizes run 0.1, 0.2, 0.45 and 0.65 µm. Applications include protein
   purification, blood filtration, cell cultures and pharmaceutics.

Q: ptfe, hydrophobic
A: PTFE is hydrophobic with excellent chemical resistance - the best choice for aggressive,
   non-aqueous solvents, acids, bases and gas/venting applications. As a cartridge it is
   validated as a sterilising-grade filter (ASTM D6394 SP0112) for sterile gas filtration,
   bioreactor off-gassing, autoclave and tank vents. Pre-wet for aqueous samples.

Q: nylon, ny
A: Nylon membranes give broad chemical compatibility for both aqueous and organic solvents -
   a solid general-purpose choice for HPLC. Note higher protein binding, so avoid where
   protein recovery matters.

Q: pes, polyethersulfone, polyethersulphone
A: PES offers fast aqueous flow with very low protein binding and low adsorption - excellent
   for aqueous buffers, biological samples and fermented beverages. Cartridge versions are
   FDA-compliant with a stainless-steel ring for steam sterilisation, pore sizes 0.2-0.8 µm.

Q: cellulose acetate, ca
A: Cellulose Acetate (CA) membranes are hydrophilic with very low protein binding - well
   suited to aqueous biological samples.

Q: mce, mixed cellulose, cellulose esters
A: Mixed Cellulose Esters (MCE) membranes are an economical choice for routine aqueous
   filtration and microbiology, with high retention capacity.

Q: cellulose nitrate, nc, blot, protein affinity
A: Cellulose Nitrate (NC) membranes have excellent protein affinity and high blocking
   capacity - popular for Southern, Northern and Western blot transfer methods.

Q: membrane list, what membranes, materials, which membranes
A: Dorsan membranes: Cellulose Acetate (CA), Mixed Cellulose Esters (MCE), Cellulose Nitrate
   (NC), Nylon (NY), Polycarbonate (PCTE), Polyethersulfone (PES), Polypropylene (PP),
   PTFE and PVDF. Tell me your solvent and I'll suggest the right one.

Q: compatibility, compatible, solvent, acid, chemical, which membrane
A: Quick membrane guide - Nylon: general HPLC, broad solvents. Hydrophilic PVDF: LC-MS,
   ultra-low extractables. PTFE: aggressive/non-aqueous solvents. PES or CN: aqueous buffers,
   high flow, low adsorption. Tell me your exact solvent and sample and I'll narrow it down.

# ---------------- CHROMATOGRAPHY ----------------

Q: hplc, uhplc, lc-ms, chromatography, column, sample prep, sample preparation
A: For chromatography, filtration is "column insurance". Use a syringe filter for final
   sample filtration (0.45, 0.22 or 0.1 µm) to protect the column and get sharp, reproducible
   peaks, and a membrane disc filter for mobile-phase filtration. Membrane picks: Nylon for
   general HPLC, hydrophilic PVDF for LC-MS, PTFE for aggressive solvents, PES/CN for aqueous buffers.

Q: why filter, baseline, peak, column life, backpressure, ghost peak
A: Unfiltered samples and solvents cause peak tailing, baseline noise, rising backpressure,
   blocked column frits and shorter column life. Proper syringe + mobile-phase filtration
   gives clean injections, stable baselines and reproducible, validated data.
